Frontend Web Developer

Back when I was an active Tumblr user, I couldn't help but mess around with the code to personalise my page's theme. After quitting my job in 2020, I knew I wanted to dig deeper into web development.

Python was my first programming language, but I wasn't sure what direction to take next. I thought about attending a bootcamp abroad, but that meant I'd have to bear the financial burden while being unemployed. So, I went on the hunt for a more suitable course and discovered a popular one on Udemy, taught by Colt Steele. What drew me in was that it catered to beginners with no prior experience, which was a major plus considering my background in Psychology.

For three months, I dedicated myself to the course, working day and night. Whenever I got stuck, I'd hit up the forum for help. Eventually, I acquired the skills to connect to a MongoDB database, retrieve data from APIs, develop a camping site in React and deploy it on Heroku.

Fast forward to now, I'm proud to say that I'm a frontend web developer at DotRoot Technologies, a position I have held for three years. I've built portfolio sites that showcase our clients' company information, and CMS platforms that let admins modify data and analyse visualisations.
